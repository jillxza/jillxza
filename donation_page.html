Support Our Furry Friends: Donate to Make a Pawsitive Impact!

Each contribution, big or small, plays a crucial role in providing shelter, care, and medical aid to our four-legged friends in need. It also empowers us to continue our advocacy for responsible pet ownership and canine welfare.

Here's where your donation will go:

Home Sweet Home

Your donation contributes to creating comfortable living spaces for our furry friends. From cozy beds to interactive toys, we strive to make our adoption center a warm and welcoming environment. Some of our dogs may need a little extra attention to learn the ropes of being the perfect companion.

Pooch Nutrients

Bark Buddy Toys

Getting our dogs to and from the adoption center or to their new homes is crucial. Your contribution helps fund safe and comfortable transportation, ensuring our furry friends travel with care.

    Eligibility Criteria for Adoption

    1. Age requirement

    Adopters must be at least 21 years old. If not, they should be accompanied by a parent or guardian.

    2. Living Arrangements

    Adopters should have stable living arrangements. If renting, confirmation from the landlord or property management allowing pets is required.

    3. Financial Stability

    Adopters must demonstrate the financial means to provide for the dog's necessities, including food, veterinary care, and other associated costs.

    4. Time Commitment

    Dogs require time, attention, and companionship. Adopters should have a schedule that allows for daily exercise, mental stimulation, and social interaction.

    5. Understanding of Responsibilities

    Adopters should have a clear understanding of the responsibilities associated with dog ownership, including feeding, grooming, training, and veterinary care.

    6. Commitment to Long-term Care

    Adopters should be prepared for the long-term commitment of caring for a dog, which can span over a decade or more.

    7. Regular Veterinary Care

    Adopters must commit to providing regular veterinary care, including vaccinations, spaying/neutering, and any necessary medical treatments.

    8. Household Agreement

    All members of the household should be in agreement with and supportive of the decision to adopt a dog.

    Eligibility Criteria for Rehoming

    1. Honesty and Transparency

    Owners must provide accurate information about the dog's health, behavior, and any special needs.

    2. Vaccination and Medical Records

    Owners should provide up-to-date vaccination records and any relevant medical history.

    3. Spaying/Neutering

    If the dog is not already spayed or neutered, owners should consider this option before rehoming, unless there are valid health reasons not to.

    4. Temperament Assessment

    Owners should have a good understanding of the dog's temperament and provide information to potential adopters for a successful match.

    5. Behavioral Training

    Owners are encouraged to invest time in basic obedience training.

    6. Health Check-Up

    Before rehoming, owners should ensure the dog is in good health by obtaining a veterinarian's assessment.

    7. Proof of Circumstances

    Owners should provide documented proof justifying their inability to care for the dog. This may include financial constraints, health issues, or other substantial reasons.
